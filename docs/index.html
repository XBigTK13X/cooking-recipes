Alton Brown's Southern Biscuits

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces ap flour
  • 4 teaspoons powder of baking
  • 1/4 teaspoon soda of baking
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 ounce butter
  • 2 ounces shortening (crisco)
  • 8 ounces buttermilk

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees
  2. Whisk together the dry ingredients
  3. Cut in the shortening and butter using a whisk until you have coarse crumbs
  4. Make a well and combine the buttermilk and the dry ingredients with a spoon
  5. Knead the dough in the bowl with your hands until no loose flour remains in the bowl
  6. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and gently knead it for a minute or so, until you have folded it and the dough is consistent
  7. Roll out the dough, then cut out as many biscuits rounds as you can, then fold the dough and repeat the process until no dough remains
  8. Place the cut out biscuits onto a biscuit sheet so that they barely touch
  9. Bake for 20 minutes

Apple Crisp

Ingredients

  • 4 apples (thinly sliced)
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3/4 cup ap flour
  • 1/3 cup butter (softened)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350
  2. Grease an 8x8x2 inch pan
  3. Spread apple slices evenly throughout pan
  4. Mix water, cinnamon, and salt in a bowl
  5. Pour cinnamon mixture over apples
  6. Mix sugar, flour, and butter in a bowl
  7. Spread crumb mixture over the apples
  8. Bake for 40 minutes or until crumb is golden brown

Apple Sauce Cake

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup shortening
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon soda of baking
  • 1 cup sour apple sauce (cold)
  • 1 and 3/4 cup ap flour
  • 2 teaspoon water (warm)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 300
  2. Cream the shortening, sugar, salt, and cinnamon
  3. Dissolve soda of baking into warm water
  4. Stir soda of baking mixture into apple sauce
  5. Mix the apple sauce mixture into the creamed base
  6. Stir in the flour
  7. Pour batter into a loaf pan
  8. Bake for 50 minutes or until the middle has set

Banana Bread

Ingredients

  • 400 grams (4-5 bananas) banana (over ripened)
  • 113 grams (1 stick) unsalted butter (melted)
  • 50 grams brown sugar
  • 150 grams white sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 235 grams ap flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon soda of baking
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 90 grams pecan
  • 90 grams chocolate chips

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350
  2. Grease a 9x5x3 loaf pan. Line it with parchment, then grease the parchment
  3. In a large bowl, mash the bananas with the butter, sugars, eggs, and vanilla
  4. In another bowl, stir together the ap flour, soda of baking, salt, and cinnamon
  5. Combine the nuts and chocolate chips, separate 1/4 of the mixture
  6. Gently fold the wet into the dry
  7. Gently fold in the 3/4 portion of mixins
  8. Pour the batter into the loaf pan
  9. Sprinkle the remaining 1/4 mixins over the top of the batter
  10. Bake until the center temps between 200 and 205 degrees (a little over an hour)
  11. Let the load rest in it's pan for 15-20 minutes, then use the parchment to move it to a cooling rack

Notes

Modified from https://cafedelites.com/banana-bread/

Chocolate Chunk Cookies

Ingredients

  • 4 ounces (1 stick) unsalted butter
  • 200 grams (1 cup) light brown sugar
  • 20 grams (1 tablespoon) molasses
  • 50 grams (1/4 cups) white sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 3 grams (1 teaspoon) vanilla
  • 250 grams (1 1/2 cups) ap flour
  • 5 grams (1 teaspoon) soda of baking
  • 4 grams (1/2 teaspoon) salt
  • 4 ounces 60%-70% high quality dark chocolate (roughly chopped)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350
  2. Brown the butter on the stove
  3. Pour the browned butter into a large bowl
  4. Whisk brown sugar, white sugar, and molasses into the browned butter
  5. Whisking the sugars should cool the butter enough that it won't scramble the eggs
  6. Whisk in egg, extra egg yolk, and vanilla
  7. Combine the ap flour, soda of baking, salt in a separate bowl
  8. Using a rubber spatula, fold the dry into the wet until just combined
  9. Fold in the chopped dark chocolate
  10. Divide dough into 18 portions (approximately 45 grams per cookie) across three lined sheet pans
  11. Try to get larger chunks of chocolate to face upward to improve final appearance
  12. Bake for 11 minutes, or until the edges start to brown
  13. Remove from the oven and place on a parchment lined cooling rack until cool

Chocolate Vanilla Swirl Cheesecake

Ingredients

  • 20 cookies oreo
  • 3 tablespoon butter (melted)
  • 32 ounces cream cheese (softened)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 4 eggs
  • 4 squares semi-sweet baking chocolate (melted)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 325
  2. Line a 13x9 baking pan with foil, leaving some foil hanging over the edge of the pan
  3. Mix cookie crumbs and butter
  4. Press crumb mixture into bottom of pan
  5. Bake for 10 minutes
  6. Beat cream cheese, sugar, and vanilla until well blended
  7. Mix in sour cream
  8. Mix eggs in one at a time
  9. Set aside 1 cup of the cream cheese batter
  10. Mix melted chocolate into remaining batter
  11. Pour chocolate batter over the cooked crust
  12. Drop spoonfuls of set aside batter into the pan
  13. Cut through the batters multiple times using a dull knife to create a marbling effect
  14. Bake for 40 minutes or until the center is almost set
  15. Cool and then refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight

Crab Rangoon

  • 1/2 teaspoon chili paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder

  • 1 pinch black pepper

Steps

Crab Rangoon Dipping Sauce

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup rice vinegar
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup
  • 2 teaspoons red chile flakes or chile paste
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 1 tablespoon corn starch
  • 1/2 cup pineapple chunks

Steps

  1. Combine vinegar, brown sugar, ketchup, chili flakes, and water in a small saucepan and bring to a simmer over medium-high heat, whisking to combine until sugar is dissolved
  2. Combine remaining tablespoon water and corn starch in a small bowl and whisk with a fork to form a slurry.
  3. Whisk slurry into the sauce and bring to a boil.
  4. Add the pineapple chunks and hit the whole pot with in immersion blender

Notes

Taken from Serious Eats

Crepe

Ingredients

  • 2 large egg
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter (melted)
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla

Steps

  1. Whisk the eggs and milk together
  2. Add the rest of the ingredients and whisk until smooth
  3. Refrigerate at least one hour
  4. Heat a 10 inch nonstick skillet over medium
  5. Lightly grease with butter, wipe off excess
  6. If the batter is too thick to pour, thin it out with either milk or melted butter
  7. Hold the pan and pour in 1/3 cup of batter
  8. Immediately swirl the batter to evenly coat the bottom in a paper thin layer
  9. Cook until the top look dry, should take around 20 seconds
  10. Grab one edge of the crepe and cook on the second side for 10 seconds, or until edges start to brown

Notes

Based on Kenji's crêpe recipe

Diner Style Pancakes

Ingredients

  • 150 grams (1 cup) ap flour
  • 2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon soda of baking
  • 1/2 teaspoon powder of baking
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 4 tablespoon butter (melted)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Mix all of the dry ingredients together.
  2. Preheat a griddle to 325
  3. Beat the egg white, buttermilk, egg, melted butter, and oil
  4. THoroughly mix the wet ingredients into the dry.
  5. Using a paper towel, spread a pad of butter on the griddle to create a barely visible coating of fat
  6. Pour the desired pancake size onto the griddle
  7. Flip the pancakes once the bottom edge starts to brown
  8. Serve immediately

Notes

The dry mix can be prepared long in advance and left to sit on a shelf in a container until the day comes when you want pancakes.

Easy Oreo Cheesecake

Ingredients

  • 18 ounces oreo sandwich cookies
  • 1/4 cup butter (melted)
  • 32 ounces cream cheese (softened)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 4 eggs

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 325
  2. Pulse 30 oreo cookies in a food processor until finely ground
  3. Pulse in the melted butter to form a crumb
  4. Line a 13x9 inch baking pan with aluminum foil, leave some foil hanging over the edges of the pan
  5. Firmly press oreo crust into bottom of greased baking pan
  6. Beat cream cheese, sugar, and vanilla until well blended
  7. Mix sour cream into cream cheese mixture
  8. Mix in eggs one at a time
  9. Chop remaining oreo cookies
  10. Gently fold 1 and 1/2 cups of chopped cookies into the cream cheese mixture
  11. Pour mixture into pan with the crust
  12. Sprinkle pan with the remaining chopped cookies
  13. Bake for 45 minutes or until the center is almost set
  14. Let the cheesecake cool and then refrigerate for at least 3 hours, preferabley overnight

Linzer Cookies

Ingredients

  • 226 gram butter
  • 339 gram ap flour
  • 113 gram powdered sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 egg yolk
  • 2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon almond extract
  • 2 teaspoon (1 lemon) lemon zest
  • 12 oz seedless fruit jam (raspberry, apricot, etc)

Steps

  1. Combine the salt and flour in a bowl
  2. Cream the butter and powdered sugar together
  3. Mix egg yolk, vanilla extract, almond extract, and lemon zest into the creamed butter
  4. Fold the dry ingredients into the wet
  5. Tightly wrap the dough in plastic wrap
  6. Chill for at least two hours
  7. Remove the dough from the fridge
  8. Preheat the oven to 350
  9. Gently roll out on a floured surface to 1 cm thickness
  10. Cut the dough into 3 inch rounds
  11. Using a 1 inch cutter, remove the center from half of the rounds
  12. Reroll scraps and continue process until all usable cookies are shaped
  13. Bake the cookies for 14 minutes
  14. Move cookies to a cooling rack and allow to cool completely
  15. Spread two teaspoons of jam on each base cookie, top with the ring cookie
  16. Cookies can be enjoyed immediately, but improve with an overnight rest to allow absorption of jam moisture

New York Pizza Dough

  • 10 grams salt

  • 20 grams extra virgin olive oil
  • 280 grams water (warm)

Nilla Wafer Cookies

Ingredients

  • 1 stick unsalted butter (softened)
  • 75 grams powdered sugar
  • 50 grams white sugar
  • 70 grams milk
  • 5 grams vanilla extract
  • 130 grams ap flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees
  2. Cream white sugar and powdered sugar into the butter using a whisk attachment
  3. Mix in the vanilla and milk
  4. Using a spatula, gently stir the ap flour and salt into the creamed mixture
  5. Transfer the dough to a pastry bag and pipe into mini 1/2 inch rounds. The dough will not spread much, if at all.

  7. Remove the cookies to a cooling rack

Notes

Modified from https://bakingamoment.com/homemade-vanilla-wafers/

Pancakes

Ingredients

  • 5 ounces ap flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon powder of baking
  • 1/4 teaspoon soda of baking
  • 1/2 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1 large egg (separated)
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter (melted)
  • ounces 6 buttermilk
  • ounces 4 sour cream

Steps

  1. Preheat a griddle to 325
  2. Preheat oven to "Keep warm" setting with a cooling rack on a sheet pan
  3. Combine dry ingredients in bowl
  4. In an imersion blender column, whip the white of an egg to stiff peaks
  5. In a second glass bowl, melt the butter
  6. Stir the buttermilk, egg yolk, and sour cream into the melted butter
  7. Gently fold the whipped egg whites into the wet mix
  8. Gently combine the wet mix into the dry mix
  9. Dish pancakes out onto the griddle at desired size
  10. Once cooked through, keep warm in oven

Notes

Taken from Serious Eats.

The sour cream can be substituted 1 to 1 with more buttermilk, but the final texture won't be quite as fluffy

Peach Pie Filling

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2.5 pounds peach (sliced, frozen)
  • 100 grams white sugar
  • 100 grams brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 40 grams (4 tablespoon) corn starch
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 sprinkle nutmeg

Steps

  1. Add the frozen peaches and water to a large pan
  2. Stirring occasionally, bring the pan to a simmer and then cover
  3. While the peaches come up to temperature, combine the white sugar, brown sugar, salt, corn starch, cinnamon, and nutmeg in a large bowl
  4. Once the peaches are mostly softened, dump them into the bowl of dry ingredients and combine everything
  5. Add the lemon juice and vanilla to the mixture and combine
  6. Dump the bowl's contents back into the pan and simmer
  7. Once the filling reaches your desired thickness and the peaches reach the desired softness, and the hot filling to a prebaked pie crust

Steps

  2. Cut the shortening and butter together into the dry ingredients until they form crumbs

Pillows

Ingredients

  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup splenda zero
  • 9 oz skim milk greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups ap flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon soda of baking
  • mixins (see step 1)

Steps

  1. The mixins will determine what kind of pillow you make. My favorite is 2 cups of frozen blueberries. We have also had success with 1/2 cup of mini chocolate chips. Anything you would use to flavor a muffin should work great
  2. Preheat oven to 400
  3. In a bowl, stir the flour, soda of baking, and salt together
  4. In a separate large bowl, whisk the splenda and eggs together until splenda is evenly distributed
  5. Whisk in the vanilla, applesauce, and greek yogurt
  6. Using a spatula, gently fold the dry ingredients into the wet mix. Do not overwork the batter.

  10. Move the cooked pillows to a wire rack
  11. Once cooled, store in refrigerator for up to one week.
  12. Before serving, toast the pillow to reconstitute it.

Pizza Process

Ingredients

Steps

Notes

Making pizza at home involves a number of small steps that need to be executed perfectly.

First, I like to make a big batch of dough and freeze until needed. For a nice fluffy pie, I will use 1/2 a batch of Keni's Pan Pizza Dough. Not the 1/3rd from the recipe. Move the dough from the freezer to the fridge (still in the quart container) the day before you want to make a pie.

Two to three hours (depending on room temperature), move the fridge dough to the counter. Open the container and cover with plastic wrap.

One hour before you want to make a pizza, put a 1/2 inch pizza steel near the top of a gas oven. It should be just low enough so that a pie can slide in with clearance for the broiler. Set the temperature to 550. Put a sheet pan under the steel to catch any extra cornmeal.

While the stone is preheating, make a batch of Pizza Sauce, shred some low moisture full fat mozz, and put some pepperoni in a little dish. Having all the igredients ready makes the final steps work. If you aren't prepared, then the dough will stick to the peel.

Get a little dish of course cornmeal and a second little dish of AP Flour. Bring out your largest cutting board. Place a pizza peel on the board and top the peel with parchment paper. Flour the parchment paper. Flour a rolling pin.

Make sure the steel is hot enough to cook the pie. You want an infared thermometer to read at least 500 degrees on a couple surface points. Note that if you go above 600, then the dough will burn, so this recipe doesn't work on a grill.

Using the rolling pin, and reflouring as needed, work the dough into an even thickness round on the parchment. Once desired thickness is acheieved, sprinkle the dough with a generous coating of cornmeal. Remove the peel from under the dough. Flip the peel and put it on the cornmeal side of the dough. Use the parchment paper to quickly flip the peel and dough over, landing the cornmeal side of the dough on the peel.

Test moving the dough. If anything sticks (especially near the lip of the dough), then toss a heavy pinch of cornmeal under it. Note that when testing the dough's movement, all that should be required is a light touch. If you need to use too much force, then cornmeal is needed. Otherwise, the ingredients will slide all over the place.

Over the course of about 30 seconds, add a light amount of sauce to the dough. It should almost touch the edge of the round. Test the dough's ability to move and add cornmeal if needed. Sprinkle 1/4 pound of shreed mozzarella over the sauce. Test the dough's ability to move and add cornmeal if needed. Fill in sauce and cheese gaps with pepperoni. Do not overload on toppings. Test the dough's ability to move and add cornmeal if needed.

If the dough is mobile, then gently slide it in little flicks onto the pizza steel. Make sure to load as far back as possible to avoid the pizza overhanging the front of the steel. Turn the heat off on the oven and set the broiler to high.

Optionally set aside a couple small dipping dishes with extra sauce. These will be used for dunking the crust of each slice after eating.

The pizza will be fully cooked within 10 minutes, but go by look and smell. I often give the pie a turn or two while cooking, and switch off the broiler once the toppings reach desired doneness. The most important tell is that the bottom of the pie is taking on a nice peopard spotting appearance.

Set the pie on a cooling rack over the previously used parchment paper. Leave it here for a minute or two to allow steam to escape, locking in that crispy crust. While allowing steam to escape, sprinkle the pie with dried oregano and micrograte parm and pecorino over the top

Once ready to serve, use the peel to move the pizza back to the large cutting board. Use a sharp round pizza cutter to make the desired number of slices. With my 1/2 dough recipe, I like 6 big slices.

Previous Attempts

Note that this current list is the result of years of making pizzas in a gas oven. Every tool, ingredient, and step is essential. Leave anything out, and the entire pie will be subpar.

One major step that finalized this process was switching from parchment paper to cornmeal. Parchment paper makes getting the pie in the oven much easier. However, you need to pull it out before it lights on fire.

That little bit of time to pull the pie out and let heat escape results in a weaker rise and a less flavorful pie.

I wanted to try course semolina, but I couldn't find it anywhere around me. After trying cornmeal, I am certain all my favorite pizza joints were using cornmeal on their doughs.

You want to flour the dough as little as possible when shaping it on the peel. Otherwise it will develop a poor tasting thin layer of unhydrated flour that will make the pizza taste raw and bitter.

I have tried to make NY style super thin pizzas, but that will be a different write up. I like to roll for this method because it gives a better height across the entire pie.

Vegetarian Chili

Ingredients

  • 36 ounces soy chorizo
  • 24 ounces diced tomatoes
  • 24 ounces crushed tomatoes
  • 15 ounces tomato sauce (unseasoned)
  • 15 ounces pinto beans
  • 15 ounces black beans
  • 16 ounces kidney beans
  • 9 ounces white onion
  • 16 ounces red bell pepper
  • 16 ounces green bell pepper
  • 8 ounces celery
  • 10 ounces carrot
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 tablespoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon msg (accent)
  • 1 ounce william's original chili seasoning
  • 1 splash cooking oil (peanut)
  • 1 bag corn chip scoops (optional)
  • 1 container plain greek yogurt (optional)

Steps

  1. Dice the carrots, sweet peppers, celery, and white onion. I dice each chunk of carrot and pepper to twice the volume of the celery and onion
  2. Add the diced aromatics to a large cold pot
  3. Splash the veggies with the cooking oil
  4. Sprinkle the salt, ground black pepper, garlic powder, and MSG into the pot
  5. Give everything a stir and bring the heat up to sweat the vegetables
  6. While sweating, remove the soy chorizo from its packaging and have at the ready
  7. Once a carrot is about half way to the doneness you prefer, stir in the soy chorizo. I look for the carrot to be just barely starting to soften to a fork
  8. Open and rinse the pinto, black, and kidney beans. Skipping the bean rinse will result in an unpleasant earthy flavor in the final prepped chili
  9. After the soy chorizo is brought to temperature and the pot begins sizzling again, stir in the rinsed beans
  10. Stir in the tomato sauce, crushed tomatoes, and diced tomatoes
  11. Stir in the chili seasoning
  12. Simmer for at least ten minutes before serving
  13. Best results will be obtained by allowing the chili to cool and then storing in the fridge at least overnight
  14. This chili goes great with a side of corn chips and a dollop of plain greek yogurt
