Alton Brown's Pancakes

  • 1 cup ap flour
  • 4 tablespoon butter (melted)
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon powder of baking
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon soda of baking
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  1. Mix all of the dry ingredients together. This can be done well ahead of time and left to sit on a shelf in a container until the day comes when you want pancakes.
  2. Preheat a griddle to 325
  3. Separate one egg
  4. Beat the egg white into the buttermilk
  5. Beat the yolk into the melted butter
  6. Combine the two separate egg mixtures
  7. Barely mix the wet ingredients into the dry. It will be lumpy and look like it isn't mixed enough. Leave it alone!
  8. Using a paper towel, spread a pad of butter on the griddle to create a barely visible coating of fat
  9. Using an ice cream disher, drop pancakes onto the heated and greased griddle
  10. Flip the pancakes once the bottom edge starts to brown
  11. Serve immediately

Alton Brown's Southern Biscuits

  • 12 ounces ap flour
  • 1 ounce butter
  • 8 ounces buttermilk
  • 4 teaspoons powder of baking
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 ounces shortening (crisco)
  • 1/4 teaspoon soda of baking
  • 2 tablespoon sugar
  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees
  2. Whisk together the dry ingredients
  3. Cut in the shortening and butter using a whisk until you have coarse crumbs
  4. Make a well and combine the buttermilk and the dry ingredients with a spoon
  5. Knead the dough in the bowl with your hands until no loose flour remains in the bowl
  6. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and gently knead it for a minute or so, until you have folded it and the dough is consistent
  7. Roll out the dough, then cut out as many biscuits rounds as you can, then fold the dough and repeat the process until no dough remains
  8. Place the cut out biscuits onto a biscuit sheet so that they barely touch
  9. Bake for 20 minutes

Apple Crisp

  • 3/4 cup ap flour
  • 4 apples (thinly sliced)
  • 1/3 cup butter (softened)
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  1. Preheat oven to 350
  2. Grease an 8x8x2 inch pan
  3. Spread apple slices evenly throughout pan
  4. Mix water, cinnamon, and salt in a bowl
  5. Pour cinnamon mixture over apples
  6. Mix sugar, flour, and butter in a bowl
  7. Spread crumb mixture over the apples
  8. Bake for 40 minutes or until crumb is golden brown

Apple Sauce Cake

  • 1 and 3/4 cup ap flour
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup shortening
  • 1 teaspoon soda of baking
  • 1 cup sour apple sauce (cold)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoon water (warm)
  1. Preheat oven to 300
  2. Cream the shortening, sugar, salt, and cinnamon
  3. Dissolve soda of baking into warm water
  4. Stir soda of baking mixture into apple sauce
  5. Mix the apple sauce mixture into the creamed base
  6. Stir in the flour
  7. Pour batter into a loaf pan
  8. Bake for 50 minutes or until the middle has set

Chocolate Vanilla Swirl Cheesecake

  • 3 tablespoon butter (melted)
  • 32 ounces cream cheese (softened)
  • 4 eggs
  • 20 cookies oreo
  • 4 squares semi-sweet baking chocolate (melted)
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  1. Preheat oven to 325
  2. Line a 13x9 baking pan with foil, leaving some foil hanging over the edge of the pan
  3. Mix cookie crumbs and butter
  4. Press crumb mixture into bottom of pan
  5. Bake for 10 minutes
  6. Beat cream cheese, sugar, and vanilla until well blended
  7. Mix in sour cream
  8. Mix eggs in one at a time
  9. Set aside 1 cup of the cream cheese batter
  10. Mix melted chocolate into remaining batter
  11. Pour chocolate batter over the cooked crust
  12. Drop spoonfuls of set aside batter into the pan
  13. Cut through the batters multiple times using a dull knife to create a marbling effect
  14. Bake for 40 minutes or until the center is almost set
  15. Cool and then refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight

Easy Oreo Cheesecake

  • 1/4 cup butter (melted)
  • 32 ounces cream cheese (softened)
  • 4 eggs
  • 18 ounces oreo sandwich cookies
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  1. Preheat oven to 325
  2. Pulse 30 oreo cookies in a food processor until finely ground
  3. Pulse in the melted butter to form a crumb
  4. Line a 13x9 inch baking pan with aluminum foil, leave some foil hanging over the edges of the pan
  5. Firmly press oreo crust into bottom of greased baking pan
  6. Beat cream cheese, sugar, and vanilla until well blended
  7. Mix sour cream into cream cheese mixture
  8. Mix in eggs one at a time
  9. Chop remaining oreo cookies
  10. Gently fold 1 and 1/2 cups of chopped cookies into the cream cheese mixture
  11. Pour mixture into pan with the crust
  12. Sprinkle pan with the remaining chopped cookies
  13. Bake for 45 minutes or until the center is almost set
  14. Let the cheesecake cool and then refrigerate for at least 3 hours, preferabley overnight

Parmesan Meatballs

  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 pound ground beef (80/20)
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1 teaspoon italian seasoning
  • 3 oz parmigiano-reggiano cheese
  • pepper (to taste)
  • salt (to taste)
  1. Preheat oven to 350
  2. Beat egg in a large bowl
  3. Mix cheese into egg
  4. Mix italian seasoning, garlic powder, salt, and pepper into the bowl
  5. Barely mix the ground meats together with the contents of the bowl
  6. Form 2.5 ounce balls of meat
  7. Place the meatballs into an oven-safe pan on the stove at medium heat
  8. Once the meatballs have browned on each side, place the pan into the oven for 10 minutes
  9. Remove the meatballs once cooked, and serve immediately

Pillows

  • 2 cups ap flour
  • 2 eggs
  • mixins (see step 1)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 9 oz skim milk greek yogurt
  • 1/2 teaspoon soda of baking
  • 1 cup splenda zero
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  1. The mixins will determine what kind of pillow you make. My favorite is 2 cups of frozen blueberries. We have also had success with 1/2 cup of mini chocolate chips. Anything you would use to flavor a muffin should work great
  2. Preheat oven to 400
  3. In a bowl, stir the flour, soda of baking, and salt together
  4. In a separate large bowl, whisk the splenda and eggs together until splenda is evenly distributed
  5. Whisk in the vanilla, applesauce, and greek yogurt
  6. Using a spatula, gently fold the dry ingredients into the wet mix. Do not overwork the batter.
  7. Gently fold the mixins into the batter
  8. Using an ice cream disher, drop 12 gobs of batter onto two foiled and greased sheet pans
  9. Bake until the pillows are just starting to turn golden (15 to 20 minutes, depending on the choice of mixins)
  10. Move the cooked pillows to a wire rack
  11. Once cooled, store in refrigerator for up to one week.
  12. Before serving, toast the pillow to reconstitute it.

Rubble

  • 2 tablespoon butter
  • 2 tablespoon cocoa powder
  • 1.5 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1/4 cup dark chocolate
  • 1 cup krispy rice
  • 2 cup marshmallow
  • 2 tablespoon peanut butter
  • 1 tablespoon powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  1. Melt the butter in a pot
  2. On low heat, melt the marshmallow into the butter
  3. Melt in the peanut butter and dark chocolate
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the rice krispy
  5. Cover your fingers in coconut oil
  6. Pinch portions of dough and shape them into rough balls
  7. Combine the cocoa powder, powdered sugar, and salt in a tupperware container
  8. In batches small enough to fit in the container, add balls into the powder mix and shake to coat
  9. Refrigerate the coated balls

Sloppy Fries

  • 1/4 cup cheese blend
  • 16 ounces crinkle cut french fries (frozen)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh flat parsley (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh garlic (minced)
  • 1/8 cup ketchup
  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • 1 tablespoon light olive oil
  • 16 ounces manwich sauce
  1. Prepare the fries as instructed on the bag
  2. While the fries are cooking, brown the ground beef in a pan
  3. Once the beef is browned, add the manwich sauce to the pan. Cover and barely simmer.
  4. While the fries continue cooking, combine the olive oil, garlic, parsley, and salt and pepper to taste in a large bowl
  5. Immediately after the fries are finished cooking, coat them evenly with the oil mixture by tossing them in the bowl
  6. Plate the fries and cover with a quick zig zag of ketchup
  7. Scoop the sloppy joe over the ketchuped fries
  8. Sprinkle the cheese blend over the sloppy joe mix
  9. Give the dish another quick zig zag of ketchup

Stovetop Mac And Cheese

  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 oz cheddar cheese powder
  • 5 oz cheese blend
  • 1 oz cheese blend
  • 1/2 cup cream
  • 10 oz large elbow maccaroni (dry)
  • 1/8 cup moscato
  • 0.4 oz real bacon bits
  1. In a medium pot, bring enough water to cover the twice the pasta's height to a boil
  2. Once the water is boiling, add a heavy pinch of salt to the water
  3. Add the elbow noodles to the water and cook for 6 minutes, or until al dente
  4. Drain the noodles
  5. In the now empty pot, bring bacon bits and butter to a simmer
  6. Whisk the pot while adding the next ingredients
  7. Add the wine to the pot, bring to a simmer
  8. Add the cream to the pot, bring to a simmer
  9. Add the larger portion of cheese blend to the pot, bring to a simmer
  10. Sprinkle the cheese powder over the sauce, bring to a simmer
  11. Whisk the cheese powder until the sauce completely absorbs it, and no granules are visible
  12. Dump the cooked, drained noodles into the pot, stir until warm
  13. Let the noodles bond with the sauce, and then plate
  14. Sprinkle the smaller portion of cheese blend over the plated mac and cheese

Strawberry Swirl Cheesecake

  • 3 tablespoons ap flour
  • 3 tablespoons butter (melted)
  • 40 ounces cream cheese
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 cup honey graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/3 cup seedless strawberry jam
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla
  1. Preheat oven to 325
  2. Line a 13x9 baking pan with foil, hanging some over the edges
  3. Mix together crumbs, 3 tablespoons of sugar, and butter
  4. Press the crumb mixture into the bottom of the baking pan
  5. Beat cream cheese, remaining sugar, flour, and vanilla
  6. Mix in sour cream after other ingredients are blended
  7. Mix in one egg at a time
  8. Pour cream cheese mixture over crust in baking pan
  9. Gently drop spoonfuls of jam over the top of the batter
  10. Run a dull knife through the mixture several times to create a marbling effect
  11. Bake for 40 minutes or until the center is almost set
  12. Cool and then refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight

Tim's Chocolate Chip Cookies

  • 1 cup ap flour
  • 2 tablespoons ap flour
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup light brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoon light brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup mini chocolate chips
  • 1/2 teaspoon soda of baking
  • 2 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1/4 cup vanilla instant pudding mix
  1. Preheat oven to 350
  2. Mix flour and soda of baking in a bowl and set aside
  3. Cream the butter, light brown sugar, and sugar in a mixer
  4. Mix in pudding mix
  5. Mix in egg and vanilla
  6. Barely mix in the combined ap flour and soda of baking
  7. Gently mix in chocolate chips
  8. Using latex gloves, drop 12 balls of dough onto a nonstick cookie sheet
  9. Still with gloved hands, quickly roll each drop of dough into a smooth ball
  10. Evenly space the balls on the cookie sheet
  11. Bake the cookies for 12 minutes, or until the bottoms are barely golden brown
  12. Quickly move the cookies from the sheet pan onto a cooling rack lined with parchment
  13. Note that the cookies should appear slightly undercooked, do not put them back in the oven

Tim's Rice Krispy Treats

  • 3.2 oz butter
  • 16 oz marshmallow
  • 6.4 oz rice krispy cereal
  1. In a medium pot, melt the butter on the lowest heat setting
  2. Add the marshmallows to the pot
  3. Still on the lowest heat setting, stir the marshmallows until completely melted and mixed with the butter
  4. Take the pot off the heat, and quickly yet gently fold in the rice krispy cereal
  5. Immediately after the cereal is mixed, gently dump the mix into a greased 9 x 13 baking dish
  6. It is critical that you don't press the mix into the pan, but rather tap the mix gently to evently distribute
  7. Let the treats cool for 5 minutes, and then flip onto a greased cutting board
  8. After 30 minutes, the mix will have cooled enough for you to cut
  9. Cut the treats into 20 servings
  10. Once the treats are cool to the touch, they can be stored

Tres Leches Cake

  • cake ingredients
  • 1 box cake mix
  • 12 oz evaporated milk
  • 1 cup half and half
  • 14 oz sweetened condensed milk
  1. Follow the instructions on the box to prepare the cake
  2. Allow the cake to completely cool and leave it in the baking dish
  3. Combine the half and half, sweetened condensed milk, and evaporated milk in a bowl
  4. Use a bamboo skewer to poke holes evenly across the surface of the cake
  5. Pour the dairy mixture over the punctured cake
  6. Cover the baking dish and refrigerate at least over night

Vegetarian Chili

  • 15 ounces black beans
  • 10 ounces carrot
  • 8 ounces celery
  • 1 splash cooking oil (peanut)
  • 1 bag corn chip scoops (optional)
  • 24 ounces crushed tomatoes
  • 24 ounces diced tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 16 ounces green bell pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 16 ounces kidney beans
  • 1/2 tablespoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon msg (accent)
  • 15 ounces pinto beans
  • 1 container plain greek yogurt (optional)
  • 16 ounces red bell pepper
  • 36 ounces soy chorizo
  • 15 ounces tomato sauce (unseasoned)
  • 9 ounces white onion
  • 1 ounce william's original chili seasoning
  1. Dice the carrots, sweet peppers, celery, and white onion. I dice each chunk of carrot and pepper to twice the volume of the celery and onion
  2. Add the diced aromatics to a large cold pot
  3. Splash the veggies with the cooking oil
  4. Sprinkle the salt, ground black pepper, garlic powder, and MSG into the pot
  5. Give everything a stir and bring the heat up to sweat the vegetables
  6. While sweating, remove the soy chorizo from its packaging and have at the ready
  7. Once a carrot is about half way to the doneness you prefer, stir in the soy chorizo. I look for the carrot to be just barely starting to soften to a fork
  8. Open and rinse the pinto, black, and kidney beans. Skipping the bean rinse will result in an unpleasant earthy flavor in the final prepped chili
  9. After the soy chorizo is brought to temperature and the pot begins sizzling again, stir in the rinsed beans
  10. Stir in the tomato sauce, crushed tomatoes, and diced tomatoes
  11. Stir in the chili seasoning
  12. Simmer for at least ten minutes before serving
  13. Best results will be obtained by allowing the chili to cool and then storing in the fridge at least overnight
  14. This chili goes great with a side of corn chips and a dollop of plain greek yogurt
